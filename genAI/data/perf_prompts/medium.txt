Benchmarking local language models requires disciplined control over prompts, token limits, warmup runs, sampler settings, concurrency, device placement, and cache behavior. The operator should keep one model family constant where possible, record quantization and runtime versions, and preserve prompt text exactly across runs. Meaningful comparisons should include time to first token, steady-state token throughput, total completion time, memory pressure, and notes about quality regressions or instruction-following failures.
